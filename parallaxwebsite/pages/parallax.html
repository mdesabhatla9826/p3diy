The Mountains Throughout the Year

Winter

During the months of December through March, As winds increase, rising air in the mountains expand due to low air pressure that cools and condesnes into snow following precipitation. This creates the beautiful snow caps on top of the snowy mountains around the world. Local wildlife goes into hibernation and the mountain foliage becomes shades of brown and grey.

SPRING

Spring

In the months of March through June, the snow caps on mountains soften and slowly melt. Greenary starts to form again, the weather becomes warmer, and wildlife returns from hibernation. The ideal time to come to the mountains is back in the form of beautiful springtime views. The only issue? Pollen.

SUMMER

Summer

In the months of June through September, high elevation mountains start to cool down. Visiters are welcomed to clear, sunny views and visits from local wildlife. The pollen levels are much lower and the mountains are a prime vacation spot as the greenary and weather is near perfect.

FALL

Fall

In the months of Septermber through November, the mountains foliage starts to change into shades of red, orange, and yellow. The temperatures cool down slighltly and the local wildlife prepares to go back into hibernation. Harvests take place at local farms. The mountain views remain beautiful and the mountains remain an ideal travel spot for travelers.

And.. this cycle repeats.
